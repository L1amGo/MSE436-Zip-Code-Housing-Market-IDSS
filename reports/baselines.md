## Interpretation

The linear baseline is the hardest to beat across every metric, and it is the relevant bar because it dominates on rank correlation, the metric closest to the manager's ranking decision. It pools to a Spearman of 0.5213 and stays between 0.48 and 0.58 across all five folds, so the target carries real, learnable signal rather than noise. The two naive baselines set a low floor: naive_zero only reflects target volatility and cannot rank at all, while naive_momentum is actively harmful, posting negative rank correlation in every fold, which indicates recent 3-month changes tend to reverse rather than persist. For M2, XGBoost must clear a pooled rank correlation of roughly 0.52 to justify the added complexity, and ideally improve it in the later folds (2025-07 and 2025-08), where the linear model weakens to around 0.48 to 0.49. Beating linear on RMSE alone would not be sufficient, since ranking quality, not squared error, drives the decision the model supports.
